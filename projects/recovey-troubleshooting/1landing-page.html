Project Overview

Enterprise Infrastructure Operations Playbooks

This project documents the operational procedures, troubleshooting methodologies, recovery workflows, and service restoration practices used throughout the JCAG-LAB enterprise environment. Unlike deployment guides that focus on installing technologies, these playbooks concentrate on maintaining service availability after infrastructure components experience failures.

Every playbook follows a standardized operational methodology designed to simulate real-world break/fix operations, infrastructure support, incident response, root cause analysis, and service restoration commonly performed by enterprise infrastructure teams, data center technicians, systems administrators, and Network Operations Center (NOC) personnel.

Wherever practical and safe, failures are intentionally reproduced inside the isolated JCAG-LAB environment before being diagnosed, recovered, validated, and documented. This demonstrates not only knowledge of enterprise technologies but also the operational thinking required to safely restore production services.

Documentation Standard

Standard Scenario Structure

Every troubleshooting scenario within this project follows the same documentation format to promote consistency, repeatability, operational discipline, and standardized incident reporting.

1. Scenario Overview

High-level description of the incident and affected infrastructure.

2. Business Impact

Operational impact to users, services, and business processes.

3. Symptoms

Observable indicators reported by users or monitoring systems.

4. Initial Assessment

Immediate verification and scope determination before troubleshooting.

5. Lab Validation Procedure

Controlled failure simulation performed safely within JCAG-LAB when appropriate.

6. Diagnostic Procedure

Systematic collection of evidence to isolate the fault.

7. Root Cause Analysis

Identification of the underlying cause responsible for the failure.

8. Recovery Procedure

Step-by-step process used to restore the affected service.

9. Validation Checklist

Verification that all services have returned to normal operation.

10. Prevention & Best Practices

Recommendations intended to reduce recurrence and improve resiliency.

11. Incident Documentation

Operational records summarizing diagnostics, recovery actions, and lessons learned.

Operational Standards

Lab Validation Philosophy

Whenever practical and safe, failures are intentionally reproduced within the isolated JCAG-LAB enterprise environment. Each troubleshooting exercise emphasizes operational methodology over simply fixing the issue by documenting the complete lifecycle from failure simulation through recovery validation. Potentially destructive scenarios that could permanently damage the environment are documented as production-reference procedures rather than intentionally reproduced.

Controlled Failure Simulation

Safe failures are deliberately introduced into isolated virtual machines, enterprise services, and network infrastructure to reproduce operational incidents without affecting the underlying production-style lab environment.

  • Controlled service interruption
  • Observable failure symptoms
  • Structured diagnostic methodology
  • Repeatable recovery procedures
  • Post-recovery validation

Production Reference Scenarios

High-risk failures that may result in irreversible data loss, extensive rebuilding, or hardware damage are documented using enterprise operational procedures rather than intentionally reproduced inside the lab.

  • Hardware failure documentation
  • Disaster recovery procedures
  • Enterprise best practices
  • Operational runbooks
  • Service restoration planning
